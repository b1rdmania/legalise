# Claim Boundary

This is the public claim boundary for launch copy, README edits, landing copy, issue replies, and social posts.

Use this line:

> Legalise is open source. The hosted site is a limited evaluation environment.

---

## Safe Claims

Legalise is:

- an open-source UK legal AI workspace.
- matter-first.
- built around documents, chronology, privilege posture, modules, and audit.
- a hosted evaluation environment at legalise.dev.
- BYO-key for real AI workflows.
- Apache 2.0.
- not for live client matters.
- a reference substrate for supervised-autonomy discussions.

Safe product line:

> Open a matter. Ask the assistant. Install legal modules. Run them through capability and privilege gates. Keep the record.

Safe thesis line:

> Audit is not the product. Audit is the receipt.

Safe supervised-autonomy line:

> Legalise explores the substrate supervised autonomy would need: matter context, permissions, provenance, review gates, and accountability.

---

## Unsafe Claims

Do not claim:

- production-ready.
- live-client ready.
- regulator-grade.
- SRA-approved.
- for use on live matters.
- full WORM audit until Postgres role split is deployed.
- full data portability until export scope is complete and tested.
- hallucination solved.
- all cloud model inputs are anonymised.
- nobody else has shipped supervision.
- Legalise is a law firm.
- Legalise gives legal advice.

Avoid:

- "no one is doing this".
- "nobody in UK legal AI has shipped this".
- "regulator-grade implementation" for the open repo.
- "full audit" without caveat.
- "tamper-proof audit" before role split.

---

## What This Is Not

Use this block when space allows:

> Legalise is not a law firm. It is not legal advice. It is not for live client matters. The hosted site is a limited evaluation environment. The repo is the artefact: a matter-first workspace for testing legal modules, privilege posture, capability gates, and auditability in public.

Short version:

> Not a law firm. Not legal advice. Not for live client matters. Open source, BYO key, evaluation only.

---

## Responding To "No One Asked For Audit Logs"

Response:

> Correct. Users do not ask for audit logs. Supervisors, insurers, regulators, and partners ask for reconstructable decisions after something goes wrong. The point is not audit as a feature. The point is letting AI do more work without losing the file, the source, the permission, the reason, and the accountable human.

Short version:

> Audit is not the product. Audit is the receipt.

---

## Responding To "This Is Just Claude With A UI"

Response:

> The model is replaceable. The unit is the matter. The interesting parts are what context the model can see, which module invoked it, what capability was granted, what privilege posture applied, what it produced, and what record remains afterwards.

Do not frame the project around Claude. Claude/Anthropic is a provider detail.

---

## Responding To "Can Firms Use This Now?"

Response:

> Not on live client matters. The open repo and hosted site are for evaluation. The live-matter readiness gates are storage, durable jobs, export/delete, WORM audit hardening, key rotation, legal-quality evals, prompt shrouding, and operational deployment proof. Those are tracked in the roadmap.

## Monday Follow-Up Theme

Working line:

> In legal AI, open source is not charity. It is epistemic hygiene.

Supporting point:

> If you build entirely behind a sales deck, the incentives get strange quickly. Open source forces the architecture, assumptions, and gaps into the open before the market narrative hardens around them.
