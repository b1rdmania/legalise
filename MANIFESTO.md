# Manifesto

Legalise is built on a small set of commitments that don't change.

## Matter-first, not prompt-first.

A legal workspace organises around the matter. Every document, every prompt, every output exists in service of one client matter. AI tools that operate outside this frame aren't useful for regulated practice.

## Solicitor-in-the-loop, permanently.

Every output is a draft. A qualified solicitor reviews, verifies, and takes professional responsibility. We will never ship a feature whose default mode is to replace a solicitor's judgement. Drafts and accelerants — yes. Substitutes — no.

## Privilege-preserving by design.

Privilege isn't a feature. It's the constraint we build around. Privilege posture is a first-class matter property; local model support exists from day one; audit covers every model call so the firm can reconstruct what was disclosed, to whom, with what protection.

## UK-jurisdiction-aware, not US-shaped.

England & Wales law has its own procedural shape, its own statutory scaffolding, its own privilege model. Most legal AI is US-shaped because most legal AI capital is American. We build for UK practice on UK rules — not by translating US patterns into Anglicised vocabulary.

## Self-host without limits.

The core is Apache 2.0 forever. Self-host on any infrastructure, run any models, fork the code, modify whatever. We don't gate the matter spine, audit log, plugin bridge, or any v0.1 module behind a commercial tier.

## Boring stack, ambitious composition.

Python, FastAPI, Postgres, React. None of these will surprise anyone in 2030. The novelty lives in the composition — adversarial premortem pipelines, privilege-aware chronologies, audit-first model gateways. We reach for things that survive a decade.

## Tools that outlast the AI cycle.

Models will change. Providers will come and go. The matter spine, the audit log, the regulatory plumbing, the matter folder in markdown on disk — these survive any LLM. We optimise for the parts of the system that don't care which model you used in 2026 versus 2030.

---

This is what we won't do. Push back if we drift.
